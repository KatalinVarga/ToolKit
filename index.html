Writing Your Transcript

  • Write it exactly as you'll say it
    Word for word, like you're talking to a room of students. Keep it conversational, not like an essay.
  • Avoid idioms, jargon, and informal punctuation
    Idioms like "get across" or "figure out" do not translate well for international students or AI voice. Use clear, direct academic language. Avoid ellipses (…), exclamation marks, and brackets with stage directions.
  • Use British English throughout
    If you use ChatGPT or similar tools to help draft it, double-check and remove any American spellings, long dashes, or overly formal phrasing.
  • Do not reference specific lecture or chapter numbers
    Avoid writing "as we covered in Lecture 3" — content may be reordered or reused in different contexts. Reference topics by name instead: "as we discussed when looking at research design."
  • Aim for about>2,000 words per lecture
    (roughly 100 words = 1 minute of speaking).
  • Mark each new section with "Slide 1", "Slide 2" etc.
    so it lines up with your PowerPoint and helps production follow along.
  • Submit as a Word document (.doc/.docx)
    One file per lecture, named by chapter and lecture number (e.g. 1.5 for Chapter 1, Lecture 5).
  • Write a complete, word-for-word spoken script
    Every sentence must be written in full as if live-subtitled, as it directly drives the recorded or AI voiceover. Minor phrasing tweaks on the day are fine, but structural edits cannot be made after submission.
  • Read it aloud before you submit
    It's the easiest way to catch awkward phrasing.

Deadline: submit at least 2 weeks before your recording date.

Building Your PowerPoint

  • This PowerPoint is a storyboard for production only
    It won't be shown to students, so don't worry about design.
  • Every slide needs a clear title.
  • Use short, keyword-based bullet points
    Avoid full sentences unless it's a brief, accurately-attributed quote. Recommended: 10–12 word limit per bullet and a 6-bullet maximum per slide.
  • Recommended deck size: 20 to 25 slides
    Aim for 20–25 slides to match a 20-minute lecture.
  • Match the wording on your slide to your transcript wording as closely as possible.
  • Write in British English
    Check spelling and grammar carefully — slide text is copied straight into the final video.
  • Only use images/data you have the rights to:
    your own work, public domain, or Creative Commons (credited). Avoid copyrighted material — if there's no alternative, contact the production team before adding it.
  • Images should be high-resolution (1000px+)
    So they don't look blurry or pixelated in the video.

Deadline: submit at least 2 weeks before your recording date.

warningCopyright — please don't skip this: Avoid using copyrighted images, data, or text wherever possible. If you're not sure whether something is safe to use, contact the production team before adding it to your script or slides.
Good To Know

Roughly how the timeline works

Step 1

Agree your module structure with your Programme Manager — about 1 month before other deliverables are due.

Step 2

Submit your transcripts and PowerPoints — at least 2 weeks before recording.

Step 3

Record your voiceover and any greenscreen segments with the production team.

Step 4

Review the edited videos via Frame.io — within 2 weeks of receiving them.

Tip: the whole process usually takes 4–7 months depending on module size — staying in touch with your Programme Manager as things come up keeps everything on track.
